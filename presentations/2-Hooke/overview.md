## Topic: Food supply and population

> 13 June 2013 – The current world population of 7.2 billion is projected to increase by 1 billion over the next 12 years and reach 9.6 billion by 2050, according to a United Nations report launched today, which points out that growth will be mainly in developing countries, with more than half in Africa.

Can we feed that number of people?  This is a question that has not yet established as prominent place on the radar screens of geoscientists as one might expect, given that soil and water are two necessities of agriculture.  In this seminar session we will explore the limitations imposed by groundwater and by plant biology. 

 I suggest reading Brown first and Friedman (2009) second. 

      
## Reading List:

- Brown, L.R., 2012, Full planet, empty plates: The new geopolitics of food scarcity: W.W. Norton and Company, New York, 144 pp. (Chapters 6 and 7, p. 57 – 82). (These two chapters raise interesting questions about our ability to expand food production enough to feed a vastly increased population.) 
- Friedman,  T.L., 2009, Hot, flat, and crowded: Why we need a green revolution - and how it can renew America.  Picador/Farrar, Straus and Giroux, New York, 516 pp. (p 174, first paragraph) (This paragraph gives a nice concise explanation of how global warming will change precipitation patterns.)
- Long, S.P., Zhu, X-G., Naidu, S.L. and Ort, D.R., 2006, Can improvement in photosynthesis increase crop yields? Plant, Cell and Environment, v. 29, p. 315-330. (This is a detailed analysis of the biophysical factors that limit crop yields.)
- Madramootoo, C.A., 2012, Sustainable groundwater use in agriculture. Irrigation and drainage, v. 61 (Suppl. 1), p. 26-33. (Madramootoo  describes the groundwater problem, taking, however, a somewhat more optimistic view than Lester Brown.)
- Friedman, T.L., 2014, WikiLeaks, Drought and Syria. New York Times Op-Ed Column, 22 January, 2014. (In this piece, Friedman draws a connection between lack of water and the initiation of the Syrian conflict.)
